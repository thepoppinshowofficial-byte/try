The Conferences

Eastern vs Western Conference

The NBA's 30 teams are split into two conferences of 15 teams each, further divided into three divisions. The top 6 from each conference make the playoffs — seeds 7–10 enter the play-in.

Stats 101

NBA stats every fan should know

Modern basketball is driven by data. Here are the three metrics that every serious fan should understand before reading any analysis.

📐

Player Efficiency Rating (PER)

A per-minute stat that rates a player's overall contribution. The league average is always 15.0. Stars typically post 20+, while elite MVP-level seasons reach 30+.

🎯

True Shooting % (TS%)

Measures shooting efficiency accounting for two-pointers, three-pointers and free throws. Far more accurate than basic field goal % for comparing scorers across eras.

🔄

Usage Rate (USG%)

The percentage of team plays used by a player while on the court. High-usage players shoulder more offensive responsibility — useful context when evaluating scoring numbers.

Common questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How many teams are in the NBA?+
The NBA has 30 teams split into two conferences — 15 in the Eastern Conference and 15 in the Western Conference. Each conference has three divisions of five teams each.
How does the NBA playoff system work?+
The top 6 teams from each conference qualify directly for the playoffs. Seeds 7–10 enter the play-in tournament to compete for the final two spots per conference. The playoffs then consist of four rounds of best-of-7 series, ending with the NBA Finals.
Where can I watch NBA games in the USA?+
NBA games air on ESPN, ABC, TNT and NBA TV in the United States. NBA League Pass lets you stream every game live. Amazon Prime Video holds rights to select games. Our Schedule page has a full TV guide for every broadcaster.
What is the shot clock in basketball?+
The shot clock gives the offensive team 24 seconds to attempt a shot that hits the rim. If they fail, possession is turned over. Introduced in 1954, it is the rule that makes NBA basketball so fast-paced and high-scoring compared to other levels of the game.
How do I get started with fantasy basketball?+
Create a free account on ESPN, Yahoo Sports or NBA.com, start or join a league, then participate in a draft where each manager selects real NBA players. Your team earns points based on those players' real weekly statistics. Read our Fantasy Guide for a full strategy breakdown.
