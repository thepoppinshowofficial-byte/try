Stats explained

Three stats every NBA fan should know

Understanding these three metrics will transform how you follow the game — and help you dominate your fantasy league.

📐

Player Efficiency Rating (PER)

A per-minute stat that rates a player's all-round contribution. League average is always 15.0. Stars typically post 20+. Elite MVP-level seasons reach 30+.

🎯

True Shooting % (TS%)

Shooting efficiency that accounts for twos, threes and free throws together. Far more useful than basic field goal % for comparing scorers across teams and eras.

🔄

Usage Rate (USG%)

The share of team possessions a player uses while on the court. Essential context when evaluating scoring numbers and deciding whether a player is truly efficient.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many teams are in the NBA?+
The NBA has 30 teams split into two conferences — 15 in the Eastern Conference and 15 in the Western Conference. Each conference is divided into three divisions of five teams each.
How does the NBA playoff system work?+
The top 6 teams from each conference qualify directly for the playoffs. Seeds 7 to 10 enter the play-in tournament for the final two spots per conference. The playoffs then run as four rounds of best-of-7 series, ending with the NBA Finals.
Where can I watch NBA games in the USA?+
NBA games air on ESPN, ABC, TNT and NBA TV in the United States. NBA League Pass lets you stream every game live. Amazon Prime Video holds rights to select games throughout the season.
What is the shot clock in basketball?+
The shot clock gives the offensive team 24 seconds to attempt a shot that hits the rim. If they fail, possession turns over. Introduced in 1954, it keeps NBA basketball fast-paced and high-scoring.
How do I start playing fantasy basketball?+
Create a free account on ESPN, Yahoo Sports or NBA.com, join a league and participate in a draft. Your team earns points based on your selected NBA players' real weekly statistics. Our analysis section covers weekly fantasy tips throughout the season.
