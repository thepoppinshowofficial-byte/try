What is the NBA?

The National Basketball Association (NBA) is the premier professional basketball league in the United States and Canada. Founded in 1946, it now consists of 30 franchises split equally between the Eastern Conference and the Western Conference. The NBA season runs from October through June, ending with the NBA Finals — a best-of-7 series between the East and West champions.

Basketball is played on a rectangular hardwood court with a hoop at each end set 10 feet off the ground. Two teams of five players compete to score more points than the other across four 12-minute quarters.

The key rules — explained simply

24

Shot Clock

The offensive team has 24 seconds to attempt a shot that hits the rim. Failure to do so turns the ball over to the opposition. It is the rule that keeps NBA basketball fast-paced.

3

Point Values

Shots inside the three-point arc score 2 points. Shots beyond the arc score 3 points. Free throws, awarded after certain fouls, are worth 1 point each.

6

Fouls Per Player

Each player is allowed 6 personal fouls before being disqualified from the game. Teams enter the penalty (free throws awarded) after the opposition commits their 5th team foul in a quarter.

8

Seconds Rule

Once a team gains possession in the backcourt, they have 8 seconds to advance the ball past the half-court line. Failure results in a turnover. Keeps teams from stalling with the ball.

Each team fields five players on the court at any given time. While modern NBA basketball is increasingly positionless, understanding the traditional roles helps you follow the game.

How a game is structured

An NBA game consists of four 12-minute quarters. If the score is tied at the end of regulation, the game goes to a 5-minute overtime period — and continues with additional overtime periods until one team leads at the buzzer.

  • Tip-off: A jump ball between two players at center court starts each game and each overtime.
  • Halftime: A 15-minute break between the 2nd and 3rd quarters.
  • Timeouts: Each team gets 7 timeouts per game — coaches call these to draw up plays or stop momentum.
  • Reviews: Coaches can challenge one call per game. Officials can also review certain plays in the last two minutes.

How the NBA season works

The NBA season is one of the longest in professional sports. Here is how it unfolds from October to June every year.

🏀
October – April

Regular Season

Each of the 30 teams plays 82 games — 41 home and 41 away. Teams face each conference opponent multiple times throughout the season. Every game counts toward the standings.

⚡
April

Play-In Tournament

Teams ranked 7th through 10th in each conference compete in a mini-tournament to fill the final two playoff spots per conference. One of the most exciting new additions to the NBA calendar.

🏆
April – June

NBA Playoffs

16 teams (8 per conference) compete in four rounds of best-of-7 series. The top seed faces the 8th seed, 2nd faces 7th, and so on. You must win 4 games in a series to advance.

🥇
June

NBA Finals

The Eastern Conference champion faces the Western Conference champion in the ultimate best-of-7 series. The winner receives the Larry O'Brien Championship Trophy — the most coveted prize in professional basketball.

Common basketball terms

  • Turnover: When the offensive team loses possession without attempting a shot — via a bad pass, a stolen ball or stepping out of bounds.
  • Double-double: When a player scores double figures in two statistical categories (e.g. 20 points and 10 rebounds).
  • Triple-double: Double figures in three categories — usually points, rebounds and assists. A sign of an elite all-around player.
  • Fast break: When a team quickly advances the ball to get an easy scoring opportunity before the defense can set up.
  • Pick and roll: An offensive play where a player sets a screen (pick) on the ball handler's defender, then rolls toward the basket to receive a pass.
  • Flagrant foul: An unnecessarily hard or dangerous foul. Flagrant 1 results in free throws and possession. Flagrant 2 means automatic ejection.
